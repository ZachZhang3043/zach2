Chess History Chess is a strategic game that is played with two people. The objective of the game is to check mate your opponet, this is achieved by attacking the king and at the same time the king has no where to go and no pieces to block with. Chess can be played as a hobbie or as a serious game that could potential become your job. Chess players like Magnus Carlsen has dedicated their whole lives on the game and is making a living out of chess. To this day chess is a globally enjoyed sports with fans around the world.

List of Professinal certified chess competition 1.World Chess Championship: The most respected title in chess, featuring a match between the reigning world champion and a challenger. 2.Chess Olympiad: A biennial team event where countries from around the world compete. 3.Candidates Tournament: A tournament to determine the challenger for the World Chess Championship title. 4.FIDE World Cup: A knockout-style tournament that also serves as a qualifier for the Candidates Tournament. 5.Tata Steel Chess Tournament: A honoured annual tournament held in the Netherlands. 6.Grand Slam Chess Final: A series of tournaments featuring some of the world's top players. 7.Sinquefield Cup: Part of the Grand Chess Tour, featuring some of the world's top players. 8.Grenke Chess Classic: An elite chess tournament held annually in Germany. 9.Shamkir Chess: Another strong chess tournament held in Azerbaijan. 10.Chess World Cup: A knockout-style tournament with significant prize money and World Chess Championship qualification at stake. 11.Chess Grand Prix: Part of the FIDE Grand Prix series, with World Chess Championship qualification on the line. 12.Chess Candidates Tournament: The event that determines the challenger for the World Chess Championship title. 13.Chess Olympiad: The biennial team competition for national teams, with both men's and women's events. 14.World Youth Chess Championship: A prestigious event for young chess players under the age of 18. 15.World Junior Chess Championship: A tournament for the world's top junior players under the age of 20. 16.World Senior Chess Championship: A competition for senior chess players. 17.National Championships: Held in many countries, these events determine the national chess champions. 18.Online Chess Championships: With the rise of online chess platforms, there are various online championships and events. 19.Local and Club Tournaments: These are held at the community and club levels and cater to players of all skill levels. 20.Simultaneous Exhibitions: Often organized by grandmasters, these events involve a grandmaster playing multiple games simultaneously against amateur players. 21.Chess960 (Fischer Random) Tournaments: Tournaments where the starting positions of the pieces are randomized, creating unique and challenging games. 22.Chess Olympiad for the Disabled: A competition for disabled chess players, following the same format as the regular Chess Olympiad.
